PHYBench: Holistic Evaluation of Physical Perception and Reasoning in Large Language Models

PHYBench is a benchmark for evaluating the physical reasoning capabilities of large language models.

Abstract

We introduce PHYBench, a novel, high-quality benchmark designed for comprehensively evaluating the complex reasoning capabilities of current large language models (LLMs).

PHYBench consists of 500 innovative and meticulously curated physics problems based on real-world physical scenarios, highlighting the ability of models to understand and reason about realistic physical processes. The benchmark spans mechanics, thermodynamics, optics, electromagnetism, modern physics, and advanced physics, with difficulty levels ranging from high school to college physics competitions. Additionally, we propose the Expression Edit Distance (EED) Score, a fine-grained evaluation metric based on the edit distance between mathematical expressions, which effectively captures differences in model reasoning processes and outcomes beyond traditional binary scoring methods.

Using PHYBench, we evaluate contemporary LLMs and compare their performance with human evaluations provided by physics students from top universities. Our results clearly indicate that state-of-the-art reasoning models significantly underperform compared to human experts, highlighting the substantial limitations and future opportunities for improvement in complex reasoning scenarios.
